what are transformations?

A figure can be moved from one position to another on a flat surface by sliding (translating), turning (rotating) or flipping it over (reflecting), or by a combination of such movements. These and other kinds of movements are also called transformations.

A swing or turn, also called a rotation

The object is swung (rotated) clockwise or anticlockwise around a point called the centre of rotation. It is as if you hold the object on a string.

In its new position, the figure is called the image of the original figure. In the diagrams above, the original figures are blue and the images are yellow. Slides, turns and flips do not change the shape or size of a figure. Hence, in these transformations, the original figure and its image are always congruent.
